# Trading

The trading screen is designed to teach the workflow safely before it is ever allowed to move real
capital.

<h2 id="order-types">Order Types</h2>

The current alpha focuses on the basic order-entry concepts users expect:

- side selection
- pair context
- size entry
- notional estimation
- reference price display

These foundations matter because even a simulation-first product should train users on realistic
workflow structure.

<h2 id="human-in-the-loop">Human-in-the-Loop</h2>

CryptoDesk AI does not auto-execute AI signals. The user remains in control of every action.
Trading views and AI advisories are connected by context, not by hidden automation.

<Callout title="No autonomous trading" tone="warning">
The product goal is explainable assistance, not AI-driven execution. Human review is part of the
system design.
</Callout>

<h2 id="risk-management">Risk Management</h2>

Trading-related surfaces are paired with visible safety guardrails:

- simulation-first defaults
- explicit risk messaging
- fat-finger protection concepts
- separation between advisory flows and order submission intent

This is especially important in a fintech context, where clarity is a safety mechanism.
