# Dashboard

The dashboard is the fastest way to understand the current product posture: real-time market
visibility, AI transparency, and security messaging all live together here.

<h2 id="market-overview">Market Overview</h2>

The dashboard surfaces live or near-live market context through the validated exchange and market
store pipeline. In the alpha flow, that means users can see price movement, market direction, and
key summary information without jumping directly into execution.

Typical widgets include:

- last traded price
- 24-hour change
- market pair context
- quick links into related trading and simulation flows

<h2 id="ai-metrics">AI Metrics</h2>

The AI metrics block is deliberately transparent. It highlights items such as:

- reviewed signals
- accuracy rate
- filtered rate
- advisory posture rather than automation claims

<Callout title="Why this matters" tone="info">
AI metrics exist to keep the user informed about system behavior. They are not a promise of
profitability and should never replace judgment or risk controls.
</Callout>

<h2 id="why-it-matters">Why It Matters</h2>

The dashboard is where the project philosophy becomes visible:

- the app can feel useful before it feels dangerous
- AI can feel informative without pretending to be autonomous
- market data and trust messaging can coexist without cluttering the experience
